The Problem

Employees had stories.
They just couldn't write them.

The biggest drop-off point in previous GVH cycles: the blank text box. Employees who volunteered regularly didn't apply — not because they lacked impact, but because writing about themselves felt hard.

😓
Before Jarvis

Employees faced an open-ended application form. Many said "I don't know how to write my story" and gave up. HR spent weeks chasing submissions and editing rough drafts manually.

🤖
After Jarvis

A structured conversation guides applicants step-by-step. Jarvis interviews, validates, suggests framing — then outputs a complete, submission-ready email. No blank page. No writing anxiety.

PM Design Decisions Behind Jarvis

  • Structured the conversation as a journalist-style interview (not a form) — because people answer questions naturally but freeze at blank boxes
  • Built Jarvis-side validation and reframing mid-conversation — e.g. "your beach cleanup has a long-tail effect, the content keeps spreading after the event" — to help users see their own story's value
  • Designed the final output as a copy-ready email with exact subject line and send-to instructions — reducing zero-friction to absolute minimum
  • Handled the team vs. individual application split with different conversation paths, including team member identification and role clarification
  • Added a photo usage rights checkbox within the flow — legal requirement embedded naturally without feeling like a legal form
